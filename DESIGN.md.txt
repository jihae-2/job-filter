# Design System Strategy: The Architectural Recruiter
 
This design system moves away from the "commodity" look of standard job boards. Instead of a cluttered grid of boxes, it treats career discovery as a high-end editorial experience. We leverage a sophisticated blue-on-white palette, but we elevate it through structural layering, "No-Line" boundaries, and a hierarchy that prioritizes cognitive ease and professional authority.
 
---
 
### 1. Creative North Star: The Curated Workspace
The "Curated Workspace" philosophy views the interface not as a list of data, but as a physical environment. We replace the rigid, "boxed-in" feel of traditional UIs with a system of overlapping surfaces and intentional white space. The goal is "Professional Flow"—an interface that feels as organized as a premium architectural portfolio.
 
**Key Deviations from Standard UI:**
- **Asymmetric Composition:** Hero sections and listing layouts should avoid perfect symmetry to create a dynamic, editorial feel.
- **Tonal Boundaries:** We never use lines to separate content; we use light.
- **Textural Depth:** Subtle gradients and glassmorphism replace flat fills to add "soul" to the professional atmosphere.
 
---
 
### 2. Colors & Surface Logic
 
We utilize a sophisticated range of blues and neutrals to define the hierarchy. The interaction between `surface` and `surface-container` is the foundation of the system’s "physicality."
 
#### The "No-Line" Rule
Explicitly prohibited: 1px solid borders for sectioning. Boundaries must be defined solely through background color shifts. 
*   **Example:** A job search sidebar should use `surface-container-low`, sitting directly against a `surface` background. The transition between these two tones is the boundary.
 
#### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as stacked sheets of fine paper.
- **Base Layer:** `surface` (#f8f9fb)
- **Secondary Sections:** `surface-container-low` (#f3f4f6)
- **Actionable Cards:** `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) for maximum "pop."
- **Nesting Logic:** An inner "Quick Apply" modal should use `surface-container-highest` to feel closest to the user.
 
#### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
To avoid a "flat template" look:
- **Floating Navigation:** Use `surface-container-lowest` with an 80% opacity and a `backdrop-filter: blur(12px)`.
- **Primary CTAs:** Instead of a flat `primary` fill, use a subtle linear gradient from `primary` (#003d9b) to `primary_container` (#0052cc) at a 135-degree angle. This adds a "jewel-like" quality to actions.
 
---
 
### 3. Typography: The Editorial Voice
 
We pair **Manrope** (Display/Headline) with **Inter** (Body) to balance character with utility.
 
| Role | Token | Font | Size | Weight/Intent |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hero Title** | `display-lg` | Manrope | 3.5rem | Bold, authoritative. Use for major headings. |
| **Job Title** | `headline-sm` | Manrope | 1.5rem | Semi-bold. The primary focal point of a listing. |
| **Secondary Info** | `title-sm` | Inter | 1.0rem | Medium. Used for company names and locations. |
| **Body Copy** | `body-md` | Inter | 0.875rem | Regular. Optimized for long-form job descriptions. |
| **Metadata** | `label-md` | Inter | 0.75rem | All-caps or tracking +5% for "Salary" or "Posted Date." |
 
---
 
### 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering
 
Traditional shadows are often "dirty." This system uses **Ambient Shadows** and **Tonal Stacking**.
 
- **The Layering Principle:** To lift a job card, place a `surface-container-lowest` (pure white) card on a `surface-container-low` background. The color contrast creates the lift.
- **Ambient Shadows:** For "floating" elements like dropdowns or mobile navigation, use: `box-shadow: 0 12px 40px rgba(25, 28, 30, 0.06)`. Note the low opacity (6%) and large blur—it should feel like a soft glow, not a dark smudge.
- **The "Ghost Border" Fallback:** If a border is required for accessibility, use `outline-variant` at 20% opacity. 100% opaque borders are strictly forbidden.
 
---
 
### 5. Components & Interaction Patterns
 
#### Cards & Listings
- **Rule:** No divider lines between list items.
- **Execution:** Use `24px` vertical spacing between cards. Each card uses `surface-container-lowest` with a `lg` (0.5rem) corner radius.
- **Hover State:** On hover, a card should shift from `surface-container-lowest` to a subtle gradient of `primary_fixed` to `surface_container_lowest`.
 
#### Buttons (Primary & Secondary)
- **Primary:** Gradient-filled (Primary to Primary-Container), `xl` (0.75rem) rounded corners. Text is `on_primary`.
- **Secondary:** `surface-container-high` background with `primary` text. No border.
 
#### Form Inputs
- **Static State:** `surface-container-low` background with a `Ghost Border` (outline-variant @ 15%).
- **Active State:** Background shifts to `surface-container-lowest`, border becomes `primary` (2px).
 
#### Search & Filter Chips
- **Selection Chips:** Use `full` (pill) rounding. Unselected: `surface-container-high`. Selected: `primary` with `on_primary` text.
 
---
 
### 6. Do’s and Don’ts
 
#### Do
*   **Do** use asymmetrical layouts (e.g., a wide column for job details and a narrow sticky column for the application form).
*   **Do** use `tertiary` (#7b2600) sparingly for high-urgency highlights like "Closing Soon" or "New Match."
*   **Do** lean into `surface-bright` for headers to create a crisp, clean start to the page.
 
#### Don’t
*   **Don’t** use `primary` for anything other than the most important action on the screen.
*   **Don’t** use black (#000000). Use `on_surface` (#191c1e) for all text to maintain a premium, soft-contrast feel.
*   **Don’t** use `sm` or `none` roundedness for cards. This system requires `lg` or `xl` to maintain an approachable, modern professional vibe.
*   **Don’t** use standard "Blue 500" defaults. Always reference the specific `primary` (#003d9b) or `surface_tint` to maintain the architectural tone.
